================================================================================
MAJOR TOPICS COVERED
================================================================================

1. CYLINDER AND CONE ANALOGY
   - Primary sources: Gellius NA 7.2, Cicero De Fato 42-43
   - Demonstrates Chrysippus' compatibilism
   - Distinguishes external causes from internal nature
   - 84 passages extracted

2. LAZY/IDLE ARGUMENT (Argos Logos)
   - Classic objection to Stoic fate
   - "If it is fated that X will happen, it will happen regardless of what you do"
   - Chrysippus' refutation using confatalia
   - 152 passages extracted

3. CONFATALIA (Co-fated Events)
   - Chrysippus' distinction between simple and conjoined events
   - Example: "You will recover" vs. "You will recover IF you call a doctor"
   - Key to refuting fatalism
   - 7 passages extracted

4. MODAL LOGIC
   - Chrysippean definitions of necessary, possible, impossible
   - Connection to fate and determinism
   - 30 passages extracted

5. STOIC FATE THEORY
   - Fate as eternal causal chain
   - Heimarmenē (εἱμαρμένη)
   - Providence and determinism
   - 40 Chrysippus passages

6. ASSENT AND MORAL RESPONSIBILITY
   - Synkatathesis (συγκατάθεσις) - assent
   - Eph' hēmin (ἐφ' ἡμῖν) - that which depends on us
   - In nostra potestate (Latin equivalent)
   - Compatibilist account of responsibility
